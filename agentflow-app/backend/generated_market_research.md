## Key Insights
1.  **Packaging Design Influences Consumer Perception and Preference**
    Packaging design elements, including transparency, color, and label design, can consistently alter consumer attention levels and sensory preferences for products like Not From Concentrated (NFC) orange juice [3]. Packaging color is a dominant cue that influences consumers' health perceptions, as they rely on color as an explicit signal for health, triggering associated beliefs and mental simulations [4].

2.  **Optimization of Food Formulations for Enhanced Quality**
    Response Surface Methodology (RSM) with a Central Composite Design (CCD) can effectively optimize ingredient formulations for food products, leading to superior sensory and textural characteristics [2]. For instance, an optimized Mohanthal formulation achieved enhanced flavor, color, and consistency, with quadratic models demonstrating high predictive capabilities for key attributes [2].

3.  **Factors Affecting Quality and Stability in Pickled Products**
    The hardness and fracturability of lightly-pickled cucumber decrease over time, following a zero-order kinetic model [1]. Specific microorganisms, such as *Acinetobacter*, *Naganishia*, and *Rhodotorula*, are strongly correlated with quality loss and the development of off-flavors in cucumbers during the later stages of the pickling process [1].

4.  **Functional Ingredients for Innovative Food Development**
    Black garlic particles (BGP) can be utilized as a natural pigment and functional ingredient in low-fat innovative mayonnaise, improving its emulsifying and functional quality [5]. The incorporation of BGP, along with low methoxyl pectin (LMP) from sources like jackfruit peels or citrus, can enhance viscosity and oxidative stability of the mayonnaise [5].

## Methodology Overview
A laboratory experiment involving eighty-one individuals combined physiological measurements, such as eye-tracking to assess attention levels, with sensory evaluation to rate overall taste satisfaction for Not From Concentrated (NFC) orange juice [3]. Response Surface Methodology (RSM) with a Central Composite Design (CCD) was employed to standardize and characterize Mohanthal by optimizing its ingredient formulation [2]. Additionally, a systematic review was conducted, synthesizing 20 empirical studies to examine the influence of packaging color on consumer perceptions of healthfulness [4].
